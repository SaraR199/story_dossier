## Critical Issues Found

### Issue 1: Cassia's Council Timeline Contradiction
**Severity:** Critical
**Location:**
- Character Concept (02-character-concept.md, Cassia Nightshade section, lines 66-70)
- World Concept (03-world-concept.md, Betrayal Mechanisms section, line 127)

**Description:**
The character concept states Cassia "was a mid-level clan member during his father's reign and helped Draven consolidate power after the assassination—a service that bought his trust and her current position" on the council.

However, the world concept states: "Cassia Nightshade, who has sat on Draven's council since his father's reign, evades scent-based lie detection..."

These are contradictory statements:
- Character Concept: Cassia was NOT on the council during Draven's father's reign; she only earned a council position AFTER helping Draven consolidate power post-assassination
- World Concept: Cassia HAS been on the council since Draven's father's reign (meaning she was on it before the assassination)

**Impact:**
This contradiction affects the internal consistency of Draven's trust issues and Cassia's backstory. The character concept version (earning position after assassination) is narratively stronger because it:
1. Avoids making Draven appear incompetent (why would he trust a council member who served during the betrayal that killed his father?)
2. Creates dramatic irony (her help during his vulnerable time earned trust she now exploits)
3. Makes the timeline clearer (three years of betrayal is more believable than 10+ years)

The world concept version contradicts the careful resolution work done in the character consistency phase.

**Recommendation:**
Change the world concept to align with the character concept. Modify line 127 in the world concept from:
"Cassia Nightshade, who has sat on Draven's council since his father's reign..."

To:
"Cassia Nightshade, who earned her council position by helping Draven consolidate power after his father's assassination..."

This preserves the resolution logic from the character consistency phase while maintaining the explanation of how she evades detection.

## Minor Issues / Author Awareness Items

### Issue 1: Isla's Gift Understanding Level
**Severity:** Minor
**Location:**
- Story Concept (01-story-concept.md, line 8)
- Character Concept (02-character-concept.md, line 43)

**Description:**
The story concept describes Isla as possessing a gift "so rare even she doesn't fully understand it." However, the character concept establishes she's been using this gift professionally for years, "carving out a niche authenticating artifacts for supernatural clients" and building a career as an appraiser.

There's slight tension between "doesn't fully understand it" and "successfully uses it as professional expertise." She clearly understands it well enough to monetize and build a reputation, but doesn't understand its true nature (dragon heritage) or full potential.

**Recommendation:**
Not a breaking issue, but author should be aware of the nuance. Consider distinguishing between:
- **Functional understanding:** She knows how to use the gift (touch object, receive visions, interpret history)
- **Origin/nature ignorance:** She doesn't know WHY she has it, what it means, or that it's dragon heritage
- **Scope limitation:** She doesn't realize its full potential until awakening intensifies it

This is already implied but could be made more explicit in deep phase character development.

### Issue 2: Blood Moon Timing Thematic Opportunity
**Severity:** Minor (Enhancement Opportunity)
**Location:**
- Character Concept (02-character-concept.md, Draven background, line 18-19)
- World Concept (03-world-concept.md, Blood Moon section, line 147)

**Description:**
The world concept establishes that blood moons occur "once every decade" and the current one is "approximately three weeks" from story start. Draven's character concept states he inherited leadership at age 25 when his father was assassinated, then "spent the next decade" building the clan (making him approximately 35 now).

This means the LAST blood moon occurred around the time of his father's assassination—a potentially significant thematic connection that isn't explored in the lightweight concepts.

**Impact:**
Not a contradiction, but a missed thematic opportunity. Blood moons amplify dragon power, awaken dormant abilities, and activate powerful artifacts. Was his father's assassination coincidence, or was the timing deliberate? Did the killer use the blood moon's magical surge?

**Recommendation:**
For deep phase development, consider whether to:
1. Explore this connection (assassination timed to blood moon adds intrigue)
2. Explicitly dismiss it as coincidence (avoiding plot complexity)
3. Leave it ambiguous (mysterious backstory enrichment)

This could deepen Draven's emotional stakes around the current blood moon beyond just the crown authentication.
