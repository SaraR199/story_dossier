## Issue 1: Cassia's Council Timeline Contradiction

### Issue Identification

**Severity:** Critical
**Type:** Cross-file timeline contradiction
**Location:**
- Character Concept (02-character-concept.md, lines 66-70)
- World Concept (03-world-concept.md, line 133 in Betrayal Mechanisms section)

**Description of Contradiction:**

The character concept established that Cassia Nightshade:
- Was a **mid-level clan member** during Draven's father's reign (NOT on the council)
- **Helped Draven consolidate power AFTER** his father's assassination
- **Earned her council position** as a reward for this service

However, the world concept originally stated:
- Cassia "has sat on Draven's council **since his father's reign**"

These statements are mutually exclusive and created a logical impossibility regarding when Cassia joined the council.

### Impact Analysis

This contradiction affected multiple story elements:

**1. Draven's Character Consistency:**
- If Cassia was on the council during his father's reign, it creates a plot hole: Why would Draven trust a council member who served during the very betrayal that killed his father?
- This contradicts his established trauma and trust issues

**2. Cassia's Betrayal Arc:**
- The character concept version creates superior dramatic irony: she helped him during his most vulnerable time, which earned trust she now exploits
- A three-year betrayal timeline (since helping him) is more believable than 10+ years of undetected treachery

**3. Internal Logic:**
- The character concept version was carefully resolved during the character consistency phase to address timeline and motivation issues
- The world concept version contradicted that resolution work

### Resolution Applied

**File Modified:** 03-world-concept.md
**Section:** Betrayal Mechanisms & Internal Threats
**Line Number:** 133

**Original Text (Contradictory):**
```
Cassia Nightshade, who has sat on Draven's council since his father's reign, evades scent-based lie detection...
```

**Revised Text (Now Consistent):**
```
Cassia Nightshade, who earned her council position by helping Draven consolidate power after his father's assassination, evades scent-based lie detection because she genuinely believes she's saving the clan from what she perceives as Draven's dangerous leadership; her self-deception combined with Draven's emotional blind spot regarding those who helped him claim his position allows her to operate undetected
```

### Why This Solution Was Chosen

**1. Preserves Character Consistency Resolution:**
- The character concept version was deliberately chosen during the character consistency phase
- Changing it would undo resolved work and reintroduce previously fixed issues

**2. Narratively Stronger:**
- Creates dramatic irony (trust earned through help now exploited)
- Makes Draven's blind spot logical and character-driven (emotional debt to those who helped him)
- Provides clearer timeline (three years is more plausible for undetected betrayal)
- Avoids making Draven appear incompetent

**3. Maintains World Logic:**
- The revised world concept text now includes the explanation for how Cassia evades detection (self-deception + Draven's blind spot about helpers)
- This integration makes the world concept MORE detailed and functional, not less

**4. Thematic Alignment:**
- Reinforces the loyalty/betrayal theme through the specific irony of trust earned through loyalty service
- Deepens Draven's wound (those he rewarded for helping him become his betrayers)

### Verification of Fix

**Cross-Reference Check:**

**Character Concept (lines 66-70):**
> "She was a mid-level clan member during his father's reign and helped Draven consolidate power after the assassination—a service that bought his trust and her current position"

**World Concept (line 133) - NOW READS:**
> "Cassia Nightshade, who earned her council position by helping Draven consolidate power after his father's assassination, evades scent-based lie detection..."

**Alignment Status:** ✓ COMPLETE - Both documents now tell the same story

**No New Contradictions Introduced:**
- The fix maintains all other world concept details about betrayal mechanisms
- Dragon sense limitations (self-deception evading detection) remain intact
- Draven's blind spot is now more specifically defined and character-driven
- Timeline is consistent across all documents
