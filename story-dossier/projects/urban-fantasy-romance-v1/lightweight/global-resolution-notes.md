## Issue 1: Kira's Discovery Timeline

**Original Problem**: Kira worked as a Seattle PD detective for several years, witnessing and occasionally intervening in supernatural incidents. Yet the Covenant Guard—which actively recruits True-Seers because they're exceptionally rare and valuable—only found her five months ago. This timeline created a credibility gap: why did competent recruiters take years to locate someone who was witnessing supernatural events?

**Files Affected**:
- 02-character-concept.md (Kira's background and True-Sight manifestation)
- 03-world-concept.md (True-Sight mechanics and Guard recruitment practices - context only, no edits needed)

**Solution Applied**: Added clarifying sentences to Kira's core identity explaining that her True-Sight manifested inconsistently—activating primarily under stress, adrenaline, or intense investigative focus, then fading to background awareness she could ignore. This intermittent nature made her ability nearly impossible for the Guard to detect through their usual recruitment methods, which rely on identifying consistent supernatural perception.

**Specific Edit Location**: 02-character-concept.md, lines 8-9 (Kira's Core Identity section)

**Rationale**: This solution strengthens multiple story elements simultaneously:
1. **Explains the timeline gap logically**: Intermittent True-Sight that activates under stress is much harder to detect than constant perception
2. **Adds depth to Kira's pre-Guard experience**: Her ability to sometimes dismiss what she saw as hallucinations becomes more plausible if it wasn't always active
3. **Creates character-specific uniqueness**: Most True-Seers have constant perception; Kira's stress-triggered variant makes her an anomaly even among the rare
4. **Supports her investigative background**: True-Sight activating during intense case work aligns with her detective career
5. **Maintains world consistency**: The Guard's recruitment methods work for typical True-Seers; Kira's variant simply evaded standard detection

**Files Edited**: 02-character-concept.md

## Issue 2: Captain Thorne's Daughter - Recognition Logic Gap

**Original Problem**: Captain Thorne's daughter had True-Sight but died young because "the Guard found her too late to protect her from supernatural retaliation." This creates powerful emotional stakes for Thorne's relationship with Kira, but raises a logic question: How could a Guard Captain with deep knowledge of True-Sight fail to recognize the ability in his own daughter and protect her?

**Files Affected**:
- 02-character-concept.md (Captain Thorne's backstory and motivation)
- 03-world-concept.md (True-Sight manifestation patterns - context only, no edits needed)

**Solution Applied**: Added clarifying sentence establishing that Thorne joined the Covenant Guard AFTER his daughter's death, driven by guilt and the need to prevent other families from suffering the same loss. He wasn't part of the organization when she manifested True-Sight, and by the time he understood what had happened to her, it was too late.

**Specific Edit Location**: 02-character-concept.md, lines 106-107 (Captain Thorne's Function section)

**Rationale**: This solution strengthens character and thematic elements:
1. **Resolves the logic gap**: Thorne couldn't use Guard resources to protect his daughter because he wasn't Guard yet
2. **Deepens his motivation**: His entire career is driven by a specific tragedy, making his dedication to Kira more meaningful
3. **Adds emotional complexity**: Thorne sees Kira as a second chance precisely because he failed the first time without the knowledge/resources he has now
4. **Supports thematic exploration**: His guilt about the past informs his protectiveness, making Kira's eventual betrayal of his trust more devastating
5. **Maintains character consistency**: A man who joined the Guard specifically to prevent True-Seer deaths is exactly the person who would take Kira's safety seriously
6. **Creates irony**: The institution he joined to prevent tragedies may be the very thing that threatens Kira

**Files Edited**: 02-character-concept.md

## Issue 3: Marcus Assignment Timing and Strategic Logic

**Original Problem**: Marcus has been a fugitive for approximately 12 months, yet Kira—who has only been with the Guard for 5 months and just completed basic training—receives the assignment to capture the "most wanted fugitive" and "most effective field agent" as her first major solo assignment. The timeline raises questions: What was the Guard doing for the first 7 months? Why assign such a critical capture to a rookie?

**Files Affected**:
- 01-story-concept.md (Marcus as Kira's first assignment - context only, no edits needed)
- 02-character-concept.md (David Cross's resentment about the assignment, justification for why Kira gets it)
- 03-world-concept.md (Guard competence and operations - context only, no edits needed)

**Solution Applied**: Added explanation that multiple experienced teams have failed to capture Marcus for the past year precisely because he's a master at glamour-based evasion techniques—he can walk past operatives in plain sight, disguise his magical signature, and slip through detection grids. Kira's True-Sight represents a tactical breakthrough: she can see him regardless of his countermeasures, making her the first real threat to his freedom in twelve months.

**Specific Edit Location**: 02-character-concept.md, lines 124-125 (David Cross's Function section)

**Rationale**: This solution strengthens multiple narrative elements:
1. **Justifies the assignment strategically**: Kira isn't chosen despite being a rookie; she's chosen because her specific ability solves a problem that's defeated experienced operatives
2. **Validates Marcus's competence**: He hasn't been evading capture through luck; he's been using expert-level magical countermeasures
3. **Heightens the stakes**: If Marcus has defeated every attempt to catch him for a year, the confrontation with someone who can actually see him becomes more significant
4. **Supports David's resentment logically**: He's right that experience matters, but wrong that ability doesn't—making his wounded pride more understandable
5. **Creates tactical parity**: Both protagonist and antagonist are exceptionally skilled in complementary ways (his glamour mastery vs. her True-Sight)
6. **Adds urgency**: The conspiracy needs to eliminate Marcus before Kira's ability breaks through his defenses

**Files Edited**: 02-character-concept.md

## Issue 4: Kill-Order Justification and Operational Security

**Original Problem**: When Kira corners Marcus, a Guard tactical team arrives with kill-on-sight orders rather than attempting capture. This makes sense from the conspiracy's perspective (they want Marcus silenced), but creates an operational security problem: Marcus is accused of stealing classified magical artifacts. How does the conspiracy justify executing him instead of capturing him to recover the artifacts? Wouldn't rank-and-file operatives question orders that eliminate any chance of artifact recovery?

**Files Affected**:
- 02-character-concept.md (warehouse confrontation scene, kill-on-sight orders)
- 03-world-concept.md (conspiracy goals and Guard operations - context only, no edits needed)
- 01-story-concept.md (Marcus accused of stealing artifacts - context only, no edits needed)

**Solution Applied**: Added explanation that the official justification provided to most Guard operatives is that Marcus has been "corrupted by exposure to forbidden artifacts" and poses an existential threat requiring immediate neutralization. The conspiracy has framed him as magically compromised and too dangerous to capture alive.

**Specific Edit Location**: 02-character-concept.md, lines 76-77 (Forced Proximity section)

**Rationale**: This solution addresses operational logic while adding depth:
1. **Provides plausible cover story**: "Corrupted by forbidden magic" is exactly the kind of threat that would justify lethal force in a world where magical contamination exists
2. **Protects conspiracy operational security**: Rank-and-file operatives following kill orders believe they're neutralizing a corrupted threat, not silencing a whistleblower
3. **Adds urgency to Marcus's situation**: He's not just accused of theft; he's been labeled as magically dangerous, making even well-intentioned operatives willing to kill him
4. **Heightens Kira's revelation moment**: When she sees the team leader using one of the "forbidden" artifacts Marcus supposedly stole, it reveals the hypocrisy immediately
5. **Creates propaganda element**: The conspiracy doesn't just want Marcus dead; they've built a narrative that makes his death seem necessary for everyone's safety
6. **Supports thematic exploration**: How institutions manufacture consent for violence through fear-based narratives

**Files Edited**: 02-character-concept.md

## Issue 5: David Cross's Conspiracy Connection Logic

**Original Problem**: David Cross resents Kira for receiving the Marcus Vane assignment he expected for himself. The character file suggests he "could be working for the conspiracy" or could be a genuine ally. However, there's a logic question: If David expected the assignment and was passed over, wouldn't that make him LESS likely to be part of the conspiracy? If he were a conspiracy plant, wouldn't they ensure he got the assignment to control the investigation?

**Files Affected**:
- 02-character-concept.md (David's resentment, potential conspiracy role)
- 03-world-concept.md (conspiracy structure - context only, no edits needed)
- 01-story-concept.md (conspiracy reaching "highest levels" - context only, no edits needed)

**Solution Applied**: Clarified that David is NOT actively part of the conspiracy, but his resentment and ambition make him an ideal unknowing asset. He dutifully reports Kira's suspicious behavior through proper channels, not realizing that the supervisors receiving his reports are using him to monitor a situation they orchestrated. Reframed his function to emphasize that he "represents the professional consequences of Kira's choices and the danger of well-intentioned people serving corrupt systems."

**Specific Edit Location**: 02-character-concept.md, lines 124-125 (David's Function section)

**Rationale**: This solution strengthens character function and thematic resonance:
1. **Resolves the logic gap**: David's resentment makes perfect sense if he's NOT part of the conspiracy—he genuinely believes he was passed over unfairly
2. **Creates more insidious danger**: An unknowing asset is more frightening than a knowing conspirator; David thinks he's doing his job correctly
3. **Adds moral complexity**: David isn't evil; he's a well-intentioned person whose legitimate grievances are being exploited by corrupt leadership
4. **Supports themes**: Illustrates how institutional corruption uses good people to perpetuate itself; hierarchy becomes a weapon
5. **Increases Kira's isolation**: She can't simply identify "conspirators vs. allies"; even genuine colleagues become threats through the structure itself
6. **Maintains character potential**: David can still evolve into ally or antagonist depending on story needs, but his baseline motivation is now clearer

**Files Edited**: 02-character-concept.md

## Issue 6: Fae Life-Debt Mechanics Underdefined

**Original Problem**: Sienna owes Marcus a magically enforceable life-debt that "literally" prevents her from betraying him. This creates an important reliable resource for Marcus's trust issues, but the mechanics are undefined: What does the debt compel? What are its limits? How long does it last? What constitutes payment? Without parameters, the debt could feel like an arbitrary plot device rather than a consistent world element.

**Files Affected**:
- 02-character-concept.md (Sienna's supernatural obligation to Marcus)
- 03-world-concept.md (fae rules and magic system - provided context, but character file was better location for specific debt mechanics)

**Solution Applied**: Added specific parameters for the life-debt in Sienna's character description:
- **What it compels**: Provide aid when Marcus requests it, prevent direct harm to him, can't reveal his location to enemies
- **What it doesn't control**: Thoughts, genuine loyalty, forced unlimited service
- **Limitations**: Sienna has discretion in HOW she fulfills requests and can refuse aid that would destroy her own position
- **Satisfaction conditions**: Debt will be satisfied when she saves Marcus's life in equivalent measure to how he saved hers, with exact parameters determined by fae honor rather than precise accounting

**Specific Edit Location**: 02-character-concept.md, lines 115-117 (Sienna's Function section)

**Rationale**: This solution grounds the magical obligation in consistent rules while maintaining mystery:
1. **Establishes clear parameters**: Readers now understand what the debt can and cannot do, preventing it from feeling like arbitrary plot convenience
2. **Maintains character agency**: Sienna is bound but not enslaved; she has room to interpret and negotiate within the debt's constraints
3. **Creates ongoing tension**: The debt compels cooperation but not trust or genuine loyalty, keeping their dynamic transactional and uneasy
4. **Supports soft magic approach**: Defines enough to be consistent (fae honor determines satisfaction) without requiring exhaustive rules
5. **Provides stakes**: The debt can eventually be satisfied (life for life), meaning Marcus's reliable resource isn't permanent
6. **Enables character complexity**: Sienna can help Marcus while still pursuing her own agenda, making her genuinely morally gray rather than just controlled
7. **Reinforces world consistency**: Fae are "bound by complex rules"—this specific debt exemplifies that principle with concrete mechanics

**Files Edited**: 02-character-concept.md
