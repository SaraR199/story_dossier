## Issues Found

### Issue #1: Riley's Knowledge of Mara's Lies

**Location:** 02-character-concept.md, Riley Park section (lines 158-172)

**The Contradiction:**
The character concept states two conflicting things about Mara and Riley's relationship:

1. "She's also sharp—she knows when Mara is lying and calls her on it" (line 163)
2. "Mara must lie to her closest friend for the first time in their relationship" (line 161)

**Why This Is a Problem:**
If Mara has never lied to Riley before ("for the first time in their relationship"), then Riley shouldn't have established knowledge of "Mara's tells" for lying. Knowing someone's lying tells requires having observed them lie previously—either to you or to others in your presence.

**Impact on Story Coherence:**
This creates a logical gap in Riley and Mara's backstory. It affects Riley's function as someone who "knows when Mara is lying and calls her on it" during the supernatural secrecy subplot.

**Suggested Clarification:**
Either:
- Modify to: "Mara must lie to Riley about something this significant for the first time" (acknowledging previous white lies but emphasizing the scale)
- Or explain that Riley knows Mara's tells from watching her lie to *other people*, but Mara has always been honest with Riley herself
- Or acknowledge that Mara has lied to Riley before, making this supernatural secret even more painful because it's the biggest/most sustained lie

### Issue #2: Mara's Magical Abilities Remain Undefined

**Location:** 02-character-concept.md, Mara Chen section (lines 4-50)

**The Gap:**
The character concept establishes that Mara:
- Accidentally "shatters an ancient ward" (demonstrated significant power)
- Has "dormant magical bloodlines" that have awakened
- Needs to "learn to control her abilities before they control her"
- Will receive magical training from Kieran and Toby

However, the concept never specifies:
- What type of magic she possesses
- What her abilities actually do beyond the one-time ward-breaking
- Whether her powers are related to boundary magic (given she broke a barrier)
- How her unknown father's bloodline determines her magical nature

**Why This Is a Problem:**
While personality and arc are well-defined, Mara's actual magical capabilities are crucial for:
- Understanding what she can/can't do in plot situations
- Determining why her bloodline specifically is "the key" to the weakening Veil (per story concept)
- Establishing consistent rules for her power development arc
- Creating meaningful obstacles during training sequences

The character concept mentions she uses her "photographer's eye to notice details others miss" as an investigative skill, but this is mundane observation, not magical ability.

**Impact on Story Coherence:**
Without defining her magical abilities, later phases may struggle with:
- What exactly Kieran is training her to do
- How her powers serve (or complicate) the investigation plot
- Why her specific bloodline was targeted for "engineered awakening"
- Consistency in what she can accomplish magically at different story points

**Note:** This may be intentional vagueness to be developed in deeper phases, but some specification (even "boundary magic similar to Thornborn heritage" or "latent sensing abilities") would provide necessary grounding.

### Issue #3: Inconsistent Hybrid Power Manifestation Timeline

**Location:** 02-character-concept.md, comparing Mara Chen (lines 4-50) and Tobias "Toby" Flint (lines 190-205)

**The Logic Inconsistency:**
The character concept presents two hybrids with contradictory experiences:

**Mara Chen:**
- Half-human, half-supernatural (unknown father)
- Powers remained completely dormant into adulthood
- Lived entire life as apparently mundane human
- Only awakened when she "accidentally shatters an ancient ward"
- "Never knew she had" abilities until this triggering event

**Tobias "Toby" Flint:**
- "Hybrid like Mara—human mother, supernatural father (werewolf pack lineage)"
- "Grew up navigating both worlds" (line 194)
- This phrasing indicates his powers manifested in childhood
- He was aware of his supernatural nature from youth
- Now "in his forties" with decades of experience in both worlds

**Why This Is a Problem:**
The character concept establishes these two characters as parallel cases ("hybrid like Mara") but gives them fundamentally different origin experiences without explaining the discrepancy:

- Why did Toby's werewolf heritage manifest in childhood, allowing him to grow up aware of both worlds?
- Why did Mara's magical bloodline remain completely dormant until adulthood?
- What determines whether a hybrid's powers are dormant or active from birth?
- Is this related to the type of supernatural heritage (werewolf vs. unknown)?
- Is Mara's dormancy unusual, or is Toby's childhood awareness unusual?

**Impact on Story Coherence:**
This inconsistency could create plot holes:
- If hybrids normally manifest powers in childhood (like Toby), Mara's dormancy requires explanation—supporting the "engineered awakening" plot but raising questions about how/why her powers were suppressed
- If hybrids normally have dormant powers (like Mara), Toby's childhood awareness requires explanation—was his father involved in his life? Did his mother know about the supernatural world?
- The mechanism for hybrid power manifestation affects worldbuilding consistency and could impact future story decisions

**Suggested Clarification:**
Add explanation for why these two hybrids had different experiences. Possibilities:
- Mara's powers were deliberately suppressed/hidden (supporting "engineered awakening" plot)
- Different supernatural lineages manifest differently (werewolf vs. [Mara's father's type])
- Toby's supernatural parent was present and actively introduced him to that world, while Mara's father disappeared before she was born
- Mara's case is specifically unusual, which is why it attracts Commission attention
