## Areas Requiring Manual Review

### Potential Concern 1: Protagonist Competence Calibration
**Issue:** Protagonist needs to be competent enough to contribute meaningfully to investigation, but not so competent that their cover story becomes implausible.

**Current Balance:**
- They provide pattern recognition and unofficial intelligence
- They demonstrate quick thinking and courage
- They must constantly calibrate: useful but not suspiciously expert

**Recommendation:** During plotting phase, track each investigative contribution to ensure protagonist's help feels valuable but explicable through "lucky insight" or "pattern observation" rather than "impossible knowledge."

### Potential Concern 2: Love Interest Suspicion Escalation
**Issue:** Love interest notices many inconsistencies early. Need to manage why they don't force confrontation or detention.

**Current Justification:**
- No concrete evidence, just suspicious coincidences
- Protagonist's insights prove valuable to investigation (pragmatic reason to maintain partnership)
- Growing attraction complicates objective judgment
- "Protective observation" provides legal framework for monitoring without arrest

**Recommendation:** In plotting, ensure love interest's suspicions escalate gradually with investigation progress. Each inconsistency should deepen suspicion without triggering premature confrontation.

### Potential Concern 3: Biological Suppression Limitations
**Issue:** If biological suppression is normally effective, need clear triggers for when it fails or becomes detectable.

**Current Triggers:**
- The Vanishing's realm-bridging events cause involuntary responses
- High emotion (passion, fear) risks control slips
- Sustained stress (Sanctuary proximity) makes suppression exhausting
- Protagonist's demonic heritage specifically responds to realm-crossing violations

**Recommendation:** In scene-level writing, establish clear progression of suppression erosion. Each Vanishing event should make next suppression harder, creating escalating tension toward inevitable revelation.

### Potential Concern 4: Found Family Integration
**Issue:** Document focuses heavily on romantic leads. Found family is mentioned but not deeply integrated.

**Current Status:**
- Found family is established as important to story and themes
- World provides locations where found family could form (Warren, investigation team)
- Both characters are isolated and need community beyond romance

**Recommendation:** This is appropriate for character concept phase focusing on romantic leads. Found family development should occur in subsequent plotting phases. Consider: Who forms their found family? Warren inhabitants? Fellow investigators? Sympathetic demons from enclaves?

### Potential Concern 5: Azareth's Actual Involvement
**Issue:** Heavily implied protagonist's father (Azareth) might be involved in The Vanishing, but not definitively stated.

**Current Ambiguity:**
- Protagonist fears this possibility
- Realm-bridging nature suggests demon lord involvement
- No confirmation of Azareth's specific participation

**Recommendation:** This ambiguity is feature, not bug. Maintains tension and allows plot flexibility. However, should be resolved definitively during plotting: Is Azareth actually involved, is it a different demon lord, or is protagonist's fear unfounded?
