# Character Consistency Check Report

## Critical Issues
[Issues that break logic or create plot holes - MUST be fixed]

### 1. Dante's Lie Detection Ability - Missing from Character Profile
**Location**: World Concept vs. Main Characters dossier
**Issue**: The world-concept.md file explicitly states: "Dante can sense when he's being lied to (which makes Sera's undercover work even more dangerous)." However, this bloodline gift is completely absent from Dante's character profile in main-characters.md.

**Why This Matters**: This is a significant ability that would fundamentally affect:
- How Dante initially sees through Sera's cover (Plot Ch 2)
- The tension in their early interactions
- Why Sera's "perfect" cover credentials fail to fool him
- The stakes of their verbal sparring and negotiations

**Impact**: Without this ability mentioned in his character profile, the reader won't understand a key tool Dante uses. If he can sense lies, this needs to be:
- Listed under his "notable_interests" or create a "bloodline_abilities" section
- Explained in terms of limitations (does it work on partial truths? omissions? can it be fooled?)
- Referenced in his strategic advantages

### 2. Who Raised Sera? Kael vs. Keres Contradiction
**Location**: Character Concept (lightweight) vs. Main Characters (deep)
**Issue**:
- Lightweight character-concept.md states: "**Kael raised her to be a weapon**"
- Main-characters.md states Sera "**apprenticed with Keres**" and "**Keres taught her everything**"
- Main-characters.md identifies Kael as "Sera's uncle (Elena's younger brother)" and current Draak, but doesn't describe him raising her

**Why This Matters**: These are contradictory statements about Sera's primary formative influence. Did her uncle Kael raise her after her mother's death, then send her to train with Keres? Or did Keres raise AND train her? This affects:
- Sera's relationship with her uncle (is there emotional complexity or just cold utility?)
- Whether Kael had a parental role (making his coldness more painful) or just political oversight
- The emotional weight of betraying the Nightscale clan

**Recommended Resolution**: Clarify that Kael, as her uncle and Draak, had legal guardianship after Elena's death but was emotionally absent/cold. Keres became her actual mentor/parental figure through training. This would make both statements true in different ways.

### 3. Dante's Mother's Death - Timeline Unclear
**Location**: Main Characters backstory
**Issue**: Main-characters.md states "His mother is dead (killed in suspicious circumstances when he was young)" and later mentions "he suspects his father had a hand in it but can't prove it." Romance arc Chapter 6 has him recognize a Nightscale ballad "His mother used to sing it before she died."

**Why This Matters**:
- "When he was young" is vague - was he 5? 10? 15?
- The execution he witnessed at 16 is clearly stated, but his mother's death is not
- This affects the emotional weight of his relationship with his father
- It affects whether his mother's death and the execution are connected (did one lead to the other?)

**Recommended Resolution**: Specify when his mother died and whether it's connected to his formative trauma at 16. Example: "His mother died under suspicious circumstances when he was 14, two years before he witnessed the execution that cemented his understanding of clan ruthlessness."

## Minor Issues
[Issues that could be improved but don't break the story]

### 4. Conspiracy Timeline - Needs Precision
**Location**: Plot Structure vs. World Concept vs. Character backstories
**Issue**:
- Plot structure mentions conspiracy active for "20 years"
- Sera's mother executed "16 years ago" (Sera is 28, was 12 at death)
- World concept: "Territorial Accords" signed "fifty years ago" after a "devastating clan war"
- World concept also mentions "last major clan war twenty years ago"

**Why This Could Be Confusing**: Was there a war 50 years ago (when Accords signed) AND another war 20 years ago? Or is this a typo? When did the conspiracy start relative to these events? Was Elena killed during/after the 20-year-ago war?

**Recommended Clarification**:
- The devastating war that prompted the Accords was 50 years ago (establishes the system)
- 20 years ago, there was another major conflict (perhaps when the conspiracy began manipulating events)
- 16 years ago, Elena discovered the conspiracy and was executed
This creates a logical progression of events.

**Issue**: Character-concept says "her father is a lieutenant she barely knows" but he's never mentioned again in main-characters.md or anywhere else.

- Did he defend her after her mother's death, or abandon her?
- Is he part of the conspiracy, or ignorant of it?
- Does Sera have feelings about him?

Example: "Her father, a mid-level lieutenant, distanced himself after Elena's execution to protect his own positionâ€”a betrayal Sera never forgave."

### 6. Infiltration Discovery Timeline - Minor Inconsistency
**Location**: Character Concept vs. Plot Structure Chapter 2
**Issue**:

- Plot structure Ch 1: Sera has been undercover "**three days**"
- Plot structure Ch 2: Dante has been watching her and confronts her (implies within those first few days)

### 7. Artifact vs. Boundary Stones - Needs Clarification
**Location**: Story Concept vs. World Concept
**Issue**:
- Story concept: "a mystical artifact that controls territorial boundaries between dragon clans" (singular artifact)
- World concept: "Mystical boundary stones (ancient dragon artifacts) were placed at territorial borders" (plural stones)

**Question**: Is the stolen artifact:
- One of many boundary stones? (If so, why does stealing one destabilize everything?)
- A control mechanism that operates all boundary stones? (If so, this needs to be clearer)
- Something else entirely?

**Recommended Clarification**: Specify in world-concept.md that there are multiple boundary stones at different territorial borders, but there's also a master artifact (possibly a keystone or control mechanism) that was stolen. This creates both immediate crisis (one border is vulnerable) and larger stakes (if they crack the master artifact's secrets, all borders could fail).

### 8. Nightscale Territory Description - Minor Vagueness
**Location**: World Concept vs. Main Characters
**Issue**: World concept says Nightscale territory is "Coastal city stronghold and surrounding port districts" but doesn't name the city. Main-characters.md says Sera maintains an apartment in "coastal city, port district" without naming it.

**Why This Is Minor**: The story is set in an alternate version of our world, so specific city names might not be necessary. However, grounding it in a real location (or a fictionalized version of one) could add specificity.

**Recommendation**: Either name the city (e.g., "Based in a fortress overlooking the Seattle waterfront" or create a fictional city name) OR specify that clan territories are deliberately unnamed to preserve the "hidden world" aspect. Either choice works, but consistency helps.

### 9. Training Timeline - Tight but Plausible
**Location**: Main Characters backstory
**Issue**:
- Sera started apprenticing with Keres at age 16 (per character-concept.md)
- She's now 28 (12 years of experience)
- Main-characters.md says "She spent her twenties on long-term undercover assignments"
- If she spent ages 16-20 in training/early missions, then ages 20-28 (8 years) on long-term assignments, this works

**Why This Is Minor**: The math works out, but it's tight. "Spent her twenties" could be interpreted as "most of ages 20-30" which wouldn't fit.

**Recommendation**: Specify "spent most of her twenties on long-term undercover assignments" to acknowledge she's 28 (not quite through her twenties) and that training took several years.

### 10. Keres's Gender Specification
**Location**: Main Characters - Secondary Characters
**Issue**: Secondary character profile specifies "Keres Nightscale: Master of Intelligence for the Nightscale clan and Sera's mentor. **Female**, late 50s..."

**Why This Might Be Confusing**: In earlier mentions (character-concept.md backstory), Keres is referred to with female pronouns but called "master of intelligence" (using traditionally male title). This is fine and intentional (showing dragons don't adhere to gendered titles), but initial mentions should include pronoun clarity.

**Recommendation**: This is handled well in main-characters.md. Just ensure early references make gender clear to avoid reader confusion.
